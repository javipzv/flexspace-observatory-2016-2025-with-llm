Section 1

How has the ratio of members per resource evolved?

The analysis of the ratio of members to resources in coworking spaces paints a complex picture of resource demand evolution from January 2016 to April 2025. Notably, smaller coworking environments have experienced a significant decline in their member-resource ratios, indicating a growing imbalance as these spaces struggle to accommodate increasing membership while maintaining resource availability. In contrast, larger coworking spaces enjoyed relative stability, suggesting different dynamics at play for varying membership sizes.

Geographically, the trends reveal distinct patterns across countries, with Spain showing variability in resource availability, while the UK remained mostly stable and the US demonstrated moderate fluctuations. Overall, the data highlights a worrying decline in resource availability per member in coworking spaces worldwide, emphasizing the need to understand these changing dynamics as demand continues to morph in different contexts.

Key findings

Decline in Small Space Resource Efficiency

Coworking spaces with 0-40 members saw a decline in the mean members per resource from 3.17 to 2.41, indicating reduced efficiency in resource allocation since mid-2022.

Diminished Resources Across All Regions

Overall, there has been a notable decrease in available resources per member across all regions, with smaller coworking spaces experiencing a drop in member-to-resource ratios.

Stable Demand in Larger Workspaces

In the 101-250 and +250 member categories, member-to-resource ratios remained relatively stable, suggesting that larger coworking spaces are better managing demand pressures.

Mean members per resource by location

Over the observed period from January 2016 to April 2025, the mean members per resource in coworking spaces showed notable trends across various membership sizes. Smaller coworking spaces (0-40 members) exhibited a sharp decline from 3.17 to approximately 2.41, indicating a significant weakening of demand as resources became less proportional to increasing member numbers, particularly evident from mid-2022 onwards. The 41-100 member category also experienced a decline from about 17.08 to 8.66, reflecting increasing membership pressures relative to resource availability. Conversely, larger groups (101-250 and +250 members) demonstrated relative stability amidst slight declines, suggesting that proportionality remains more manageable compared to smaller spaces.

Comparatively, geographical trends reveal different patterns of stability and volatility. Spain displayed dramatic peaks and troughs, starting at around 3.91 and reaching over 11 at its peak, while facing subsequent fluctuations and a recent recovery. The UK remained fairly stable, hovering around 16-19, with minor declines and recoveries, whereas the US experienced modest oscillations from approximately 10 to 12. Overall, the aggregated data indicates a general decline in available resources per member across all regions, with some localized resilience in specific markets, highlighting shifting dynamics in coworking space resource demand.

Section 2

How has the number of bookings evolved?

The analysis of global bookings reveals a significant recovery trend in the demand for coworking spaces as businesses adapt to flexible work arrangements post-pandemic. Notably, larger membership categories have seen a remarkable resurgence in bookings, indicating a growing preference for spacious and adaptable work environments, while smaller categories continue to struggle with volatility and challenges in their recovery.

Examining the data from various countries, we uncover contrasting recovery patterns with Spain facing fluctuations, the UK showing notable resilience, and the US experiencing a moderate rebound. Additionally, the demand for different resource types has evolved, with booths and hot desks thriving, while conference rooms decline, reflecting the shifting preferences and requirements of today’s workspace.

Key findings

Larger Spaces Surge in Popularity

Coworking spaces with over 250 members reached a peak of 412 bookings in early 2023, indicating a strong demand for larger flexible workspaces post-pandemic.

UK Leads Coworking Recovery

The UK demonstrated consistent growth in coworking bookings, peaking at around 216 in 2023, contrasting with Spain's fluctuating recovery trajectory.

Hot Desks on the Rise

Demand for Hot Desks is projected to increase steadily, rebounding to approximately 3.5 bookings per resource by 2025, reflecting a shift towards flexible work arrangements.

The analysis of global bookings for coworking spaces indicates a significant recovery trend from the pandemic across various membership sizes. The largest category of coworking spaces, with over 250 members, peaked impressively at around 412 bookings in early 2023, reflecting a burgeoning demand for larger flexible workspaces. In contrast, the smallest category (0-40 members) exhibited erratic patterns, with notable peaks and troughs influenced heavily by the pandemic—highlighting ongoing challenges in this segment despite some recovery efforts.

Comparative insights from Spain, the UK, and the US reveal varied recovery trajectories post-pandemic. Spain experienced severe fluctuations and notable seasonal declines, whereas the UK demonstrated the most resilience with a consistent growth trend, peaking at around 216 bookings in 2023. The US showed moderate recovery, stabilizing around 120 bookings after initial declines, indicating a gradual adjustment to new post-pandemic norms. Overall, while all markets are rebounding, the larger coworking segments appear to be capitalizing on the shift toward flexible work arrangements more effectively than the smaller ones.

How types of resources have evolved from 2016 to 2021

From 2016 to 2025, the demand for various resource types demonstrated distinct trends. Booths and Hot Desks showcased significant recovery and steady growth, respectively, with Booth rebounding to approximately 3.5 bookings by 2025 after a pandemic-related dip. Meeting Rooms remained consistently popular yet fluctuated, while the demand for Hot Desks increased steadily, indicating a shift towards flexible work arrangements. In contrast, the Conference Room bookings displayed a steady decline, dropping nearly 60% over the decade, reflecting changing organizational preferences. Event Spaces peaked significantly in mid-2022 but faced a notable decline afterward. Overall, while resources like Booths and Hot Desks are thriving, others such as Conference Rooms exhibit diminishing relevance in the evolving workspace landscape.

Section 3

Has COVID-19 been a turning point in resource consumption patterns?

The COVID-19 pandemic has undeniably influenced resource consumption patterns on a global scale, particularly in the realm of coworking spaces. Initial increases in booking durations were observed, showcasing a marked shift in user behavior as individuals sought longer-term arrangements in response to the evolving work landscape. This transition hints at a potential long-term change in demand dynamics as users navigate post-pandemic recovery.

As countries adapt to new norms, recovery patterns reveal a diverse landscape. Notably, regions like Spain and the UK exhibit distinct trajectories, contrasting with the stability seen in the US market. This variability in booking durations among members and non-members alike offers insightful perspectives on how customer preferences are reshaping resource consumption in the post-COVID era.

Key findings

Surge in Booking Durations

Coworking space booking durations skyrocketed by 30% in small spaces during April 2020, reflecting heightened demand amidst the pandemic.

Diverging Recovery Trends

Post-COVID, booking patterns in Spain and the UK diverged significantly, while the US maintained longer booking durations, signaling varied recovery trajectories.

Shift in Customer Behavior

Non-Members showed a substantial increase in booking lengths, pushing averages to 180 hours by late 2022, indicating changing customer preferences.

The analysis of coworking booking durations reveals a significant impact from the COVID-19 pandemic across all size categories. Initial increases in booking lengths were observed, particularly in smaller spaces, with durations jumping as much as 30% during April 2020. By late 2021, these spaces began to recover, surpassing pre-pandemic levels by achieving average booking durations of around 180 hours by the end of 2022, indicating a strong demand rebound.

Comparative trends across different countries also illustrate varying recovery patterns post-COVID, with Spain and the UK experiencing spikes around the same time, yet diverging paths afterward. The US market reflected a more stable resilience, maintaining longer booking durations, indicating a potential shift towards longer stays. Non-Members showcased even greater increases in booking lengths, suggesting evolving customer behaviors in response to the pandemic, while Members stabilized at lower averages, hinting at differing recovery trajectories between the two customer groups.

Section 4

Booking patterns by country booking based on time of the day, day of the week and month.

This section delves into the intricate booking patterns observed across Spain, the United Kingdom, and the United States, meticulously analyzing how time of day, day of the week, and seasonal trends influence resource demand. Notably, distinct cultural behaviors emerge, highlighting sharp peaks in European bookings during specific hours in contrast to more gradual trends in the U.S., framing a diverse landscape of global booking preferences.

Moreover, the analysis of weekly and monthly booking distributions reveals a predominant weekday focus, especially early in the week, with minimal weekend engagement. Seasonal variations are particularly pronounced in Spain, contrasting with the steadiness of trends in the UK and U.S. This synthesis underscores the importance of timing in understanding global booking trends and the factors driving resource demand across different regions.

Key findings

Weekday Booking Dominance

Over 22% of bookings in Spain and the UK occur on Tuesdays and Wednesdays, highlighting a clear preference for midweek reservations.

Cultural Variations in Evening Bookings

Bookings in Spain peak at 9 AM and drop nearly to zero by 8 PM, contrasting with the US’s steadier booking levels throughout the day.

Seasonal Trends in Spain

Spain experiences significant seasonal fluctuations, with winter months seeing the highest booking peaks compared to the UK's stable trend and the US's consistent performance.

Booking distribution by time of the day

The booking distribution analysis by time of day reveals significant patterns across Spain, the United Kingdom, and the United States. In Spain, bookings peak at 9 AM with a noteworthy drop-off in the evening, plunging to nearly negligible rates by 8 PM. The UK reflects a similar trend but retains slightly higher afternoon activity, while the US shows a more gradual decline. This suggests a cultural difference in evening booking behaviors, with European trends exhibiting sharper peaks and drops compared to the US's more stable distribution throughout the day.

When examining the booking patterns by day of the week, all regions demonstrate a marked preference for weekdays, particularly Tuesday and Wednesday. Spain and the UK show over 22% of bookings early in the week, while the US touches around 20% midweek. Weekend bookings are minimal across the board, affirming that weekdays are the primary focus. Monthly trends indicate that Spain experiences the highest seasonal fluctuations, with peaks in winter months and significant drops in summer, contrasting with the UK's more stable trends and the US's consistent performance throughout the year. Overall, this analysis underscores the critical timing and seasonal variations shaping global booking behaviors.

Section 5

Evolution of the average price of booking.

The evolution of the average booking prices in coworking spaces from 2016 to 2025 reveals diverse trends influenced by space size and geographical factors. Smaller spaces, catering to fewer members, exhibited considerable price volatility, particularly during the pandemic recovery phase. In contrast, larger coworking environments showed greater price stability and resilience, consistently maintaining higher average booking rates across the timeline, reflecting differing demand dynamics within these segments.

Geographical analysis further highlights significant disparities in pricing patterns. While Spain displayed notable fluctuations, the overall trend pointed upward, contrasting with the relatively stable yet higher price levels in markets like the UK and the US. This section will delve into these trends, providing insights into how market influences and competitive strategies have shaped the pricing landscape for coworking spaces over the years.

Key findings

Small Spaces Face Price Volatility

Between 2016 and 2023, smaller coworking spaces fluctuated significantly in pricing, recovering from a low of 25.92 to 32.04.

Stability in Larger Spaces

Coworking spaces with over 250 members maintained higher average prices, peaking at 42.18 in late 2024, showcasing price stability despite market fluctuations.

Geographic Pricing Disparities

Spain's coworking prices rose from 18.75 in 2016 to approximately 25.54 in 2025, contrasting with the UK and US markets which exhibited higher resilience in pricing.

Mean final hourly price

The analysis of mean final hourly prices across coworking spaces from 2016 to 2025 reveals significant price dynamics segmented by space size and geographic location. Smaller coworking spaces (0-40 members) experienced the most volatility, with prices fluctuating from 29.4 in early 2016 to around 32.04 in 2023, reflecting a recovery from a substantial low of 25.92 during the pandemic. In contrast, larger coworking spaces (101-250 members and over 250 members) demonstrated greater price stability, consistently maintaining higher mean prices throughout the period and experiencing less drastic declines, particularly evident in the +250 members category which peaked up to 42.18 in late 2024.

Geographically, coworking space prices varied noticeably, with Spain showing significant fluctuations but an overall upward trend from 18.75 in 2016 to approximately 25.54 in 2025. The UK and US markets displayed relatively higher mean prices, with the UK pricing ranging between 26 and 41 while the US consistently hovered above 30 with peaks around 41.85. Overall, while Spain exhibited the greatest price swings, UK and US markets demonstrated higher resilience, suggesting distinct demand dynamics and market influences affecting pricing trends in coworking spaces.

Percentage of zero final hourly price by space size

The analysis of zero final hourly price percentages across different coworking space sizes reveals distinct trends from 2016 to 2025. For spaces with 0-40 members, the percentage started at approximately 14.55%, peaking at around 18% in 2018, but generally fluctuated between 7% to 11% in recent years. Conversely, coworking spaces with 41-100 members saw a higher initial percentage of 14.38%, reaching peaks of over 27% by 2021, indicating more consistent pricing strategies. In contrast, those with 101-250 members demonstrated higher volatility, with significant spikes reaching over 34%, before stabilizing near 18%. Larger spaces (+250 members) displayed the most erratic behavior, with percentages frequently exceeding 30%, suggesting a competitive pricing strategy to capture market share.

Overall, the data indicates that larger coworking spaces are more likely to maintain zero final hourly prices, likely due to their capacity to manage demand and adjust pricing strategies effectively. In comparison, smaller coworking spaces tend to have lower maximum percentages and more stable pricing, which may reflect their limited operational flexibility. Overall, the trends suggest that coworking spaces are increasingly leveraging pricing strategies to attract users, particularly in the wake of market fluctuations influenced by events like the pandemic.

Conclusion

The future is flex

The global analysis of resource demand within coworking spaces reveals significant shifts in member-to-resource ratios and booking behaviors across different space sizes and geographical regions. Smaller coworking spaces, particularly those with fewer than 40 members, have struggled with declining resource efficiency, reflecting a broader trend of diminished resources available per member. In contrast, larger coworking spaces with over 250 members have proven adept at managing demand, maintaining stable member-to-resource ratios even as booking volumes surged to new heights post-pandemic. This divergence indicates that while smaller entities grapple with resource allocation challenges, larger setups capitalize on growing popularity, establishing themselves as preferred options for flexible workspaces.

The findings also underscore the evolving landscape of coworking preferences, marked by an increasing demand for Hot Desks and extended booking durations, particularly from non-members. Trends in booking behavior demonstrate regional variations, with the UK leading the recovery in coworking bookings amid fluctuating patterns in Spain and the stable tendencies observed in the US. Additionally, the pricing dynamics reveal that while small spaces face volatility, larger coworking environments achieve stability, reflecting differing market resilience. These insights hint at a potential future where larger and more adaptable coworking spaces become the predominant choice, as customer preferences shift towards flexibility and sustained engagement.
